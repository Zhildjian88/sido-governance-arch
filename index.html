Section 01
System Overview
Five-layer governance stack. Signals flow from multimodal ingestion through policy classification, enforcement state assignment, monitoring and control, to regulatory proof output. Each layer is independently operable and version-controlled.
View:
L1
Signal Ingestion & Multimodal Fusion
Ingests video frames, audio, text, metadata, account context, and virtual currency telemetry. Computer vision, audio classifiers, NLP models, and hash/dedup run in parallel. C2PA Content Credentials read at ingest. Maturity scoring applied. Output: unified risk assessment per content unit.
Computer Vision Audio Classifiers NLP Models Hash / Dedup C2PA Reader Content Levels Scoring Currency Telemetry Account Context Fusion
L2
Policy & Content Governance
Content classification and policy lookup execute in the same pipeline step. Global Community Guidelines as baseline. Local policy supplements applied by jurisdiction. Enforcement state determined. Moderation is a sub-function of governance — not a parallel track.
Policy Abstraction Layer Content Classifier Jurisdiction Router Satire / Context Engine Cluster Signal Detector Confidence Threshold Engine
L3
Enforcement & Escalation Workflow
Enforcement state machine assigns outcome. Six states: Remove, Age-Restrict, FYP Ineligible, Cluster Suppress, Label, Allow. Human escalation queues handle borderline, specialist, appeals, crisis, and LIVE moderation. Third-party integrations: C2PA, fact-checkers, law enforcement referral, cross-platform hash sharing.
Enforcement State Machine Human Escalation Queues BPO Vendor Governance Appeals Handler SoR Generator Regional Policy Overlay Financial Crime Pathway
L4
Monitoring, Drift Detection & RCA
Closed-loop control system. Observes detection outputs and enforcement metrics. Anomaly detection layer classifies root cause: policy drift, model drift, label variance, or adversarial emergence. Corrective action triggered: threshold adjustment, policy version update, model retrain, or QA calibration session.
Precision / Recall Monitor Label Quality Auditor Policy Drift Detector Model Drift Detector Adversarial Emergence Monitor RCA Engine Retraining Trigger
L5
Regulatory Proof Output
Every enforcement action generates a cited, immutable audit trail. Automated population of DSA Article 35 Transparency Report, Statement of Reasons per DSA requirements, IMDA Annual Safety Report, and law enforcement referral packages. Audit ledger is append-only and cryptographically referenced.
DSA Art.35 Pipeline SoR Auto-Generate IMDA Safety Report Audit Ledger Law Enforcement Package Financial Crime Report

// Adversarial Threat Model — Scoring Pipeline // Distinct from content-level evasion (see Section 4) // This section addresses attacks targeting the pipeline itself ATTACK SURFACE 1 — ADVERSARIAL EXAMPLES AGAINST CLASSIFIERS Threat : Imperceptible perturbations to video frames, audio, or text crafted to cause classifier misclassification at high confidence (adversarial examples) Method : Gradient-based attacks (FGSM, PGD, C&W) on visual classifiers; homoglyph / substitution attacks on NLP Impact : Content scores as low-risk despite policy violation Evades automated removal, requires human review Defence : Adversarial training — include adversarial examples in training distribution for each classifier Ensemble voting across architectures — single-model adversarial examples do not transfer reliably across different model families Ensemble methods provide ~4× robustness improvement // vs single-model detectors under adversarial conditions Input preprocessing: JPEG compression, resizing, feature squeezing to remove adversarial perturbations Certified defenses (randomised smoothing) for high-stakes categories ATTACK SURFACE 2 — PROMPT INJECTION INTO NLP CLASSIFIERS Threat : Embedded instructions in captions, descriptions, or OCR-extracted text designed to manipulate the NLP classification output Example: hidden whitespace instructions that alter model interpretation of surrounding content Method : Direct injection via caption text; indirect injection via overlaid text in video frames (OCR path) Impact : Policy violation content classified as compliant or routed to incorrect enforcement state Defence : Input sanitisation before NLP inference Separate OCR pipeline with injection detection Anomaly detection on NLP confidence distribution for content with unusual text structure patterns ATTACK SURFACE 3 — THRESHOLD GAMING VIA CONFIDENCE BOUNDARY PROBING Threat : Adversary iteratively tests content variants to map the exact confidence threshold boundary, then crafts content that scores just below the removal threshold at high precision Method : Black-box boundary probing — submit variants, observe enforcement outcome, repeat Impact : Systematic evasion of automated removal while remaining below human review routing threshold Defence : Rate limiting on upload velocity per account Threshold randomisation — add calibrated noise to routing thresholds to prevent precise mapping Confidence band monitoring: unusual clustering of content near threshold boundary → adversarial probe signal → account investigation ATTACK SURFACE 4 — MODEL EXTRACTION Threat : Adversary queries the system at scale to reconstruct a shadow model that approximates detection behaviour, then uses the shadow model offline to craft evasion content without live probing Method : Large-scale systematic upload + observation of enforcement outcomes to train surrogate model Impact : Enables offline adversarial example generation without triggering rate-limit defences Defence : Query rate limiting and velocity detection Returning soft enforcement states (e.g. reduced distribution) rather than binary remove/allow reduces signal available to extraction attacks Periodic classifier updates invalidate extracted surrogate models ENSEMBLE DEFENCE ARCHITECTURE Single model defence is insufficient against adaptive adversaries Architecture recommendation: Layer 1 : Diverse model families (CNN, Transformer, GNN) Adversarial examples rarely transfer across families Layer 2 : Temporal ensemble — models trained on different time windows of training data Layer 3 : Cross-modal consistency check — visual, audio, and text signals should be coherent; incoherence is itself a violation signal Voting : Majority vote across ensemble for high-stakes categories. Single dissenting vote → human review routing rather than automated decision KNOWN LIMITS ⚠ Adaptive adversaries with white-box access to model weights can circumvent all current defences. Assumes black-box access. ⚠ Certified defences (randomised smoothing) reduce accuracy under normal conditions — robustness/accuracy trade-off. ⚠ Ensemble methods increase inference cost ~3–5×. High-severity categories justified. All categories: cost-prohibitive. // Trade-off management: apply certified defences and full // ensemble only to Tier 1 content categories

Section 04
Monitoring, Drift Detection & RCA
Closed-loop control system. Detects four failure modes — policy drift, model drift, label variance, and adversarial emergence — and triggers targeted corrective action. Not a state machine. A feedback control loop.
Detection Outputs
Enforcement decisions, label assignments, human override events, appeal verdicts, and queue volume signals feed into the observability layer continuously.
Observed Metrics Layer
Precision and recall tracked by content category, language, and region. Label rate by category. Human override rate. Appeals reversal rate. Queue volume trends. Inter-moderator agreement rate. Category-level enforcement velocity.
Anomaly Detection Layer
Statistical monitoring of metric distributions. Drift from baseline triggers classification. Sudden spikes in false positives, label rate anomalies, or override rate shifts surface as alerts for root cause classification.
Policy Drift
Guidelines changed but enforcement logic not updated. Symptoms: rising override rate for specific category, inconsistent decisions on same content type across regions.
Model Drift
Detection model degrading over time. New content tactics evade existing classifiers. Symptoms: rising false negative rate, category-level precision drop, new content patterns not in training distribution.
Label Variance
"Anyhow label" problem. Inconsistent label application across moderators, languages, or regions. Symptoms: high inter-moderator disagreement, language-specific label rate outliers, appeals reversal clustering.
Adversarial Emergence
New evasion technique or generative tool bypassing detection. Symptoms: sudden drop in synthetic media detection rate, new content pattern with low classifier confidence, coordinated upload pattern not matching known typologies.
Threshold Adjustment
Immediate. No model retrain. Adjusts confidence routing thresholds. Deployed via config update. Logged with justification.
Policy Version Update
Policy abstraction layer updated. New version deployed. Enforcement state machine references updated policy. Historical decisions remain auditable against prior version.
Model Retrain
New training data incorporating edge cases and evasion patterns. Monthly cadence for synthetic media detection. Shadow deployment before production promotion. Calibration curves validated.
QA Calibration Session
Dedicated calibration sessions reduce inter-moderator variance. Language and region-specific calibration for consistency. Results feed back into threshold and policy updates.
Redeployment & Monitoring Reset
Corrective action deployed. Metrics baseline reset. Control loop resumes observation. All corrective actions logged to audit ledger with timestamp, RCA classification, and corrective action taken.

// Architecture Limitations Register // Documenting what the system cannot do is as important // as documenting what it can. Honest assessment only. TIER 2 — STRUCTURAL LIMITATIONS (MITIGATABLE) Cold-start problem When a novel content category emerges — new synthetic media technique, new coded language, new attack pattern — the system has no training data for that category. Detection relies on anomaly signals (distribution shift, human report spike) rather than direct classification. Gap window: days to weeks before labeled examples exist and classifier update can be deployed. ⚠ Adversaries can exploit cold-start windows deliberately by introducing novel attack patterns at low volume first to avoid triggering anomaly detection thresholds. Mitigation: Zero-shot classification using large language models as interim detection until labeled data available. Cross-platform intelligence sharing to accelerate cold-start. Threshold drift Confidence band thresholds are calibrated at a point in time. As content distribution shifts — seasonal trends, platform growth, new user demographics — the thresholds drift out of calibration without triggering the RCA system. Drift is slow and invisible until label quality collapses. Mitigation: Periodic threshold recalibration on schedule (not only event-triggered). Confidence distribution monitoring as a leading indicator of threshold drift. Conflict of interest in QA The internal QA team that audits BPO vendor quality is employed by the same platform that contracts those vendors. Structural incentive to underreport vendor failures. Mitigation (interim): Rotating external observer program. Civil society spot-audit access to QA sampling results. Published language-stratified IMA metrics. Long-term: Independent moderation quality audit standard (not yet developed at industry level). Differential privacy in model training Human review decisions feed back into model training. Those decisions are made on sensitive content. Training data retention and anonymisation governance is underspecified in current industry practice. Risk: Training data leakage, membership inference attacks against moderation models. Mitigation: Differential privacy techniques applied to training data pipeline. Formal retention and deletion policy for human review decision data. TIER 3 — SYSTEMIC GAPS (OUT OF SCOPE FOR THIS ARCHITECTURE) Incident response architecture This document models the system in steady state. It does not model failure modes at scale: novel attack breaking multiple detection layers simultaneously, false positive cascade removing legitimate content during a crisis, or regulatory emergency direction conflicting with policy. Resilience architecture and incident response are separate design domains requiring their own specification. Policy authorship governance The document has policy version control but not policy authorship governance: who can author a policy change, what approval chain is required, how civil society is consulted, what rollback looks like if a policy update causes measurable harm. Policy governance is as important as technical governance and requires a separate framework document. Whistleblower and internal escalation Moderators and internal staff who observe systematic governance failures need a protected escalation pathway. OSA has provisions here; EU Whistleblower Directive applies. Not addressed in this architecture — requires HR and legal framework, not a technical design. Independent moderation quality audit standard No industry-wide standard for independently auditing moderation decision quality currently exists. DSA delegated regulation on audits (2023) is in development. EU Commission audit methodology is not yet finalised. This is an open design problem at regulatory level, not a gap that can be closed by platform architecture alone.

Generated per enforcement action. DSA Article 17 requirement. Structure: (1) Legal / policy ground cited, (2) Factual basis documented, (3) Appeal instructions, (4) Timeline stated.
